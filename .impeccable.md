# And/or Labs — Design Context & Style

> The single source of truth for design decisions on this site. Read before any UI work.

## Design Context

### Users
Founders and operators at **seed → Series-A B2B adtech / SaaS startups**, evaluating And/or Labs as their AI-enabled GTM partner. They're often technical or technical-adjacent, time-poor, and skeptical — they've seen a hundred agency sites. They land here to answer one question: *"Can this person actually win us our category?"* The site's job is to make a credible, fast case and get them to reach out.

### Brand Personality
**Crisp · operator-credible · confident.** The voice of a 2x-exited operator who has done the work, not a marketer describing it. Earned authority, no fluff, no hedging. Dry over cute. Says things plainly ("Distribution is the new moat," "win their category") and trusts the reader.

**Emotional goal:** the founder should feel **confidence — they're in expert hands.** Every choice should reinforce "this person has done this before and will do it for me."

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Crisp Utility on White Canvas"** — the Dub system, in the lineage of **dub.co / Vercel / Linear**. Refined minimalism executed with precision: white canvas, near-black ink, a deep navy primary, and the rainbow **prism** used *only* as a thin edge accent (never a fill). Light theme, with **navy full-bleed sections** (methodology, testimonials, footer) for rhythm and emphasis against the white.

**Anti-references — what this must NOT become:**
- ❌ **Generic SaaS template** — purple-to-blue gradients, identical icon+heading+text card grids, gradient text. The #1 thing to avoid.
- ❌ **Corporate / enterprise heavy** — stock photography, stuffy institutional formality, big-consultancy energy.
- ❌ **Playful / startup-cute** — mascots, bouncy illustrations, emoji-heavy, toy-like.

The one thing someone should remember: the **prism hairline** threading the whole site, and bespoke diagram-cards that explain the method at a glance.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint is the brand.** Near-monochrome ink-on-white; the accent and prism work *because* they're rare (60-30-10 by visual weight). Adding color or decoration almost always makes it worse.
2. **The prism is a signature, never a fill.** Rainbow appears only as thin edges/dashes (kickers, card tops, dividers, footer line) — never as a background or gradient text.
3. **Every visual must earn its place by explaining, not decorating.** Diagrams are conceptual artifacts (positioning map, channel benchmark, GTM graph), not stock charts. No sparklines-as-decoration.
4. **Confidence through typography and space**, not effects. Big tight-tracked Satoshi headlines + generous whitespace carry the page. Motion is one restrained, entry-only moment per element.
5. **Operator-plain copy, verbatim from source.** Site copy comes from `Site structure.md` — never reworded, never marketing-fluffed.

## Design System (concrete reference)

### Stack
Astro 5 · Tailwind CSS 4 (`@theme` tokens in `src/styles/global.css`) · component-scoped `<style>` where needed · deployed to Cloudflare Pages.

### Type
- **Satoshi** — display / headlines (Fontshare). Bold, tight tracking, fluid `clamp()` sizing.
- **Inter** — UI & body.
- **Geist Mono** — labels, kickers, eyebrows, code-feel microcopy (uppercase, wide tracking).
- **Spectral italic** — reserved *exclusively* for the `&` brand mark. Never elsewhere. (Loaded but not yet placed — the wordmark currently reads "And/or Labs" in mono.)

**Type scale = the single source of truth** (`global.css @theme`). All sizes are **`rem`** (never `px` — respects the reader's browser font size). Each step carries its own line-height / tracking / weight, so a component writes only the token (`text-h1`) and inherits the full treatment — **never hardcode `font-size`/`tracking`/`leading` in a component.** Tracking tightens as size grows.

| Token | Size | Role | Family |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| `text-eyebrow` | 11px · 0.12em · 500 | mono uppercase kickers, plan tags, press label | mono |
| `text-caption` | 12px | fine print: testimonial roles, ©, credits | Inter |
| `text-meta` | 14px | nav, list items, names, metadata, monogram | Inter |
| `text-body` | **15px** | base body, paragraphs, card blurbs | Inter |
| `text-lede` | 17→19px (fluid, lh 1.6) | section intros / subheads, methodology body, about bio | Inter |
| `text-quote` | 18→21px | testimonial pull-quotes | Satoshi 500 |
| `text-h4` | 22px | card titles (plan name) | Satoshi 700 |
| `text-stat` | 30px | pricing figures | Satoshi 700 |
| `text-h3` | 26→36px | methodology pillar titles | Satoshi 700 |
| `text-h1` | 30→48px | section heads + the Problem thesis heading | Satoshi 700 |
| `text-display` | 40→68px | the hero | Satoshi 700 |

The hero **lede** is the one intentional bespoke size (18→22px, larger than `text-lede`) — the hero subhead earns extra presence. The Problem **thesis paragraphs** keep their own fluid size + the prism `background-clip` scroll effect — that block is a signature, never refactor it into a token.

### Palette (13 tokens — keep it tight)
| Role | Token | Value |
|------|-------|-------|
| Canvas | `--color-canvas-white` | `#ffffff` |
| Ink (text) | `--color-ink-black` | `#0a0a0a` (never pure `#000`) |
| Neutrals | `thunder-gray #171717` · `shadow-gray #262626` · `steel-gray #404040` · `muted #737373` |
| Surfaces/borders | `subtle-ash #f5f5f5` · `border-light #e5e5e5` · `border-muted #d4d4d4` |
| **Primary** (filled CTA, navy sections) | `--color-logo-navy` | `#1e1d70` |
| **Accent** (rare high-intent only) | `--color-accent-blue` | `#2563eb` (OKLCH L0.55 C0.22 H263 — deeper than blue-500, max separation from navy, AA on white) |
| Success (pricing checks/tints) | `fresh-green #16a34a` · `info-tint #dcfce7` |
| Prism signature | `--gradient-prism` | `#8cc2ff → #c8b8ff → #ffc4da → #ffddb6 → #baf0d4` (edges only) |

Don't reintroduce the trimmed vestigial tokens (warm-orange, deep-violet, highlights, etc.) — the palette is intentionally near-monochrome.

### Patterns
- **Buttons:** navy filled pill (primary) · hairline ghost pill (secondary) · `accent` variant reserved for rare high-intent CTAs. Keyboard `:focus-visible` → 2px accent ring.
- **Header:** floating translucent pill — `fixed`, centered, `max-w-760px`, `backdrop-blur-xl` + 70% white, hairline border, soft shadow. The one purposeful use of glass.
- **Sections:** `tone="white" | "ash" | "navy"`; navy sections invert text to white / white-70 and lighten dividers to `white/10`. Alternate white/navy for rhythm.
- **Prism placements:** kicker dashes · featured pricing card top · testimonial card tops · footer line · hero top bleed.
- **Motion:** scroll-entry reveals via CSS `animation-timeline: view()`, entry-only, ease-out, always with a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback.

### Content widths — exactly two indents
Every block adheres to one of two tokens (single source in `global.css` `@theme`); never introduce a third.
- `--width-wide: 1200px` → multi-column grids / full-bleed: **Methodology, Testimonials, Footer** (via `<Container>` default).
- `--width-narrow: 840px` → focused single-column: **Hero (text + logo reel), Problem, How we work, Section headings, the nav pill, About** (via `<Container width="narrow">` or `max-w-[var(--width-narrow)]`).
- Exempt (not indents): prose line-caps (lede `600px`, body `46–60ch`) and intrinsic element sizes (portrait `200px`, viz card `380px`).

### Radii & shadows
Radii 8 / 12 / 16 / 20 / full. Shadows subtle and diffused — never heavy. Spacing on Tailwind's 4pt scale; vary it for rhythm, don't pad everything equally.
